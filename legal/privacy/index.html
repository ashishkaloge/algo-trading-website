This Privacy Policy explains how tradign.com collects, uses, shares, retains, and protects information when you use the public site, authenticated workspace, AI chat, drag-and-drop strategy builder, backtests, dashboards, Telegram-style signals, exchange execution, Web3 workflows, payments, support forms, and related services.

tradign.com is software for creating, testing, deploying, and broadcasting crypto trading strategies. We are not a broker, exchange, custodian, financial adviser, investment adviser, portfolio manager, research analyst, tax adviser, or fiduciary. This policy explains data handling only; it does not change the trading-risk and no-advice terms in the Terms and Conditions.

We do not sell personal information for monetary consideration, and we do not currently share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. We use data to operate the Platform, route your instructions to third parties, secure accounts, process payments, provide support, meet legal obligations, and improve the product.

Do not provide primary-wallet private keys, seed phrases, recovery phrases, hardware-wallet PINs, exchange withdrawal codes, full passwords, full payment credentials, or any credential that grants withdrawal or transfer authority. We never need those details to provide the Platform.

1. Scope and Relationship to Third Parties

This policy covers personal information processed by tradign.com in connection with the Platform, including account access, strategy creation, AI chat, backtests, deployments, notifications, Channel Posts, billing, support, and legal acceptance.

This policy does not cover independent third parties you connect or use, including exchanges, Web3 wallets, blockchain networks, AI model providers, Telegram, email providers, payment processors, market-data providers, analytics tools, or websites we link to. Those services process data under their own policies and terms.

If you operate strategies, channels, communities, desks, or services for other people, you are responsible for your own privacy obligations to those people. Operator plan access does not make tradign.com the controller, fiduciary, adviser, broker, custodian, or publisher for your audience.

2. Information You Provide

You may provide account information such as email address, display name, Telegram username, profile preferences, plan choices, support messages, payment references, legal acceptance, and communication preferences.

You provide strategy and workspace content such as AI prompts, chat history, strategy names, project names, indicator settings, risk blocks, coin lists, timeframes, deployment settings, channel branding, watermarks, descriptions, files, screenshots, and support attachments.

You may provide connection details such as exchange API keys and secrets, exchange testnet or live environment choice, AI provider API keys, Telegram bot tokens, Telegram channel handles, email delivery settings, wallet addresses, Web3 session details, and other integration inputs. Withdrawal-enabled exchange keys, primary-wallet private keys, seed phrases, and recovery data must not be provided. A separately scoped API-wallet or agent credential may be used only through a dedicated supported signer workflow.

3. Information Generated by Platform Use

Using the Platform generates data such as strategy graph changes, AI proposal accept or reject events, backtest configurations, backtest results, deployment state, paper signals, approval decisions, direct-execution events, order attempts, sync events, errors, dashboard metrics, and plan-limit usage.

We may process local-ledger records for signals, orders, fills, cancels, slippage estimates, position state, PnL, drawdown, win rate, liquidation-risk estimates, reconciliation status, and execution errors. Exchanges and chains remain the source of truth for real balances, positions, orders, fills, and transactions.

We also collect operational telemetry such as logs, error traces, latency, feature usage, browser and device data, IP address, approximate location from IP, session identifiers, authentication events, rate-limit events, and security signals.

4. Account, Authentication, and Legal Acceptance

We use account information to create and operate accounts, authenticate users, authorize actions, maintain sessions, enforce plan access, remember preferences, provide security alerts, and support account recovery.

Authentication uses secure cookies, CSRF protections, refresh sessions, audit logs, and related security controls. Cookie and local-storage values may remember consent choices, theme, market type, workspace state, builder usage, and other UI preferences.

When Terms and Conditions acceptance is required, we store the accepted version and acceptance time. If the required version changes, the Platform may require acceptance again before authenticated use continues.

5. Strategy, Project, Builder, and Backtest Data

We process strategies, project structure, builder prompts, drag-and-drop graph edits, backtest settings, backtest outputs, saved AI strategies, selected coins, timeframes, notification settings, and dashboard analytics so you can create, compare, resume, deploy, monitor, and support your trading workflows.

Private strategies and private backtest results are treated as account-scoped content. We do not copy your private strategies into public or curated strategy catalogs without your explicit permission.

We may use aggregated or de-identified statistics, such as feature usage, accepted proposal rates, common indicator combinations, and performance of product flows, to improve the Platform. We do not use private strategy content to identify or target you outside the Platform.

6. AI Chat, Model Providers, and User API Keys

AI prompts, conversation context, strategy descriptors, repair-loop context, plan context, model responses, proposal schemas, errors, token or usage metadata where available, and acceptance or rejection signals may be processed to render chat, generate strategies, repair invalid proposals, answer platform questions, and improve the builder.

When you use AI features, relevant prompts and context may be sent to configured model providers such as Google Gemini, OpenAI, Groq, Anthropic Claude, or another provider connected through your workspace.

Tradign currently operates its AI provider tier in a mixed standard-retention mode because provider capabilities and controls differ. We disable provider-side request storage where the provider and API support a request-level control, but universal zero-data retention is not guaranteed. A provider may still temporarily process or retain request and response content for safety, reliability, abuse monitoring, legal compliance, or other exceptions under its terms and the applicable API tier.

Deleting a chat from Tradign removes or schedules removal of Tradign's copy under this policy, but cannot force a provider to delete copies it retained under its own terms or legal obligations.

If you supply your own AI provider API key (BYOK), the provider's terms, privacy settings, retention rules, and API tier for your account apply to requests made with it. The key is treated as a credential and stored through the Platform credential-vault workflow. Do not paste secrets, private keys, seed phrases, government IDs, unrelated personal data, or third-party confidential information into AI prompts.

7. Exchange API Keys and Execution Data

When you connect an exchange, we process the API key, API secret, environment, account label, masked key hint, fingerprint hash, verification status, execution account metadata, egress policy metadata, and exchange responses needed to verify and operate the connection.

Exchange API secrets are stored through vault-backed secret management and are not displayed in full after entry. Secrets are retrieved only when needed to verify credentials, sign requests, execute your configured instructions, reconcile state, or support security operations.

The expected permission model is read plus trade only. Withdrawal, transfer, master-key, account-closure, or all-permissions access must not be enabled. You remain responsible for exchange-side permissions, IP whitelisting, revocation, rotation, and monitoring.

8. Web3 and Wallet Data

Where Web3 workflows are supported, we may process public wallet addresses, chain identifiers, RPC or venue metadata, wallet connection state, signed intents, session details, token approvals, allowance metadata, balances, position state, transaction hashes, gas estimates, and smart-contract events.

We do not collect or store your primary-wallet private key, seed phrase, recovery phrase, or hardware-wallet PIN. A separately scoped API-wallet or agent credential may be processed inside an isolated signer for a supported workflow. Connected-wallet signatures and transaction confirmations occur through wallet tools you control.

Wallet addresses and on-chain transactions may be public and linkable. Once data is written to a blockchain, we cannot erase it from the chain.

9. Telegram, Channel Posts, Email, and Notifications

We process Telegram usernames, Telegram user IDs, bot tokens, bot verification status, channel handles, channel IDs, delivery settings, message content, Channel Post timing, watermark or branding settings, delivery logs, provider errors, retry state, and notification preferences.

Telegram bot tokens are credentials. A bot token can control chats or channels where the bot is an administrator, so you must revoke compromised tokens through Telegram immediately.

Telegram, email, push, and other notification providers receive message content and delivery metadata as needed to deliver messages. Once a Channel Post is published to Telegram or another third-party service, that service and your channel settings control further visibility, retention, and deletion.

10. Payments, Billing, and USDT Records

We process plan selected, billing cadence, subscription state, invoice metadata, payment status, refund or chargeback status, support references, and payment-provider identifiers needed to provide Free, Pro, Max, Operator, and future plans.

Razorpay handles INR checkout and payment-method details. We receive payment status, order or payment references, masked or tokenized payment metadata, and related reconciliation information. We do not store full card numbers, CVVs, bank passwords, UPI PINs, or full bank-account credentials.

USDT TRC20 payments are verified through transaction details you provide and public on-chain data such as transaction hash, wallet addresses, amount, timestamp, and confirmation state. Blockchain records are public and cannot be deleted by us.

11. Support Requests, Screenshots, and Attachments

Support and billing forms may include name, email, account email, plan, subject, issue type, payment method, transaction or order reference, payment date or time, message content, route context, and screenshots or image attachments.

Attachments may be emailed to the configured support inbox and retained with support records. Attach only the screenshots needed to diagnose the issue. Do not include exchange API secrets, private keys, seed phrases, full payment credentials, unrelated financial statements, government IDs, or third-party personal data unless support explicitly asks for a redacted copy.

We may review support messages, screenshots, logs, and account context to troubleshoot, prevent fraud, investigate security incidents, reconcile payments, respond to legal requests, and improve the product.

12. Cookies, Local Storage, and Tracking Choices

We use strictly necessary cookies and similar technologies for authentication, session continuity, CSRF protection, security, fraud prevention, and legal or cookie-preference records.

We use functional storage for preferences such as theme, market type, last workspace state, builder state, and UI settings. We may use analytics or performance signals to understand product usage and reliability where allowed by law and your cookie choices.

The cookie preference flow lets you accept or reject optional analytics, marketing, and personalization categories where those categories are available. Disabling essential cookies will impair or break authenticated Platform features.

13. How We Use Information

We use information to provide and operate the Platform, authenticate users, run AI chat, generate strategy proposals, run backtests, store strategies, route notifications, verify exchange or Telegram connections, process payments, provide support, enforce plan limits, and maintain legal acceptance records.

We also use information for security, fraud prevention, abuse prevention, debugging, reliability, product analytics, customer communication, compliance, tax and accounting obligations, sanctions or legal screening where required, dispute handling, and the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

Where applicable privacy law requires a legal basis, our bases may include performance of a contract, legitimate interests in operating and securing the Platform, compliance with legal obligations, consent where requested, and processing that you voluntarily initiate for the specified purpose.

14. How We Share Information

We share information only as needed for the Platform, including with cloud hosting and database providers, vault and secret-management providers, email providers, Telegram, AI model providers, market-data providers, payment processors, analytics and monitoring providers, security tools, customer-support tools, exchanges, Web3 venues, RPC providers, affiliates, and professional advisers.

We share with exchanges, chains, wallets, Telegram, AI providers, payment processors, and communication providers when you instruct or configure the Platform to use those services. Their terms and policies govern their own processing.

We may disclose information to authorities, regulators, courts, payment providers, exchanges, or other parties when we believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to comply with law, enforce terms, investigate fraud or abuse, prevent security incidents, protect users or others, or complete a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, or asset transfer.

15. Aggregated, Anonymized, and De-Identified Data

We may create aggregated, anonymized, or de-identified data from Platform usage, such as average backtest length, common builder actions, plan-limit usage, performance of product flows, or aggregate error rates.

We use this data for product analytics, capacity planning, reliability, security, business reporting, and product improvement. We do not attempt to re-identify de-identified data except where necessary to test or validate de-identification safeguards.

16. Security and Credential Protection

We use technical and organizational measures such as access controls, transport security, encryption where appropriate, secret management, role-based access, audit logging, rate limits, CSRF protection, security monitoring, and operational controls to protect Platform data.

Credentials such as exchange secrets, AI provider API keys, Telegram bot tokens, OAuth tokens, and similar secrets are handled through restricted credential workflows and masked in UI surfaces after entry. Decrypted secrets should be used only for the minimum time needed to perform the configured action.

No system is perfectly secure. You remain responsible for your devices, browsers, email accounts, exchange accounts, wallets, extensions, passwords, MFA, IP whitelisting, key rotation, token revocation, and safe handling of screenshots and prompts.

17. Retention, Deletion, and Account Closure

We retain personal information while your account is active and as needed to provide the Platform, maintain strategies and backtests, operate deployments, preserve security logs, provide support, process payments, comply with law, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Account profile and strategy content are generally retained for the life of the account unless deleted earlier. Billing, tax, fraud, support, execution, audit, and security records may be retained longer where required or appropriate. Backups may retain deleted information until normal backup rotation removes it.

You may request account closure, access, correction, export, or deletion through the Help contact form. Some information may be retained where deletion would conflict with legal obligations, security, fraud prevention, payment reconciliation, dispute handling, backup integrity, or the defense of legal claims.

18. International Processing and Transfers

The Platform, service providers, model providers, exchanges, Telegram, payment processors, and infrastructure providers may process data in countries other than your country of residence. Those countries may have different privacy and data-protection laws.

Where transfer restrictions apply, we use appropriate safeguards or rely on permitted transfer mechanisms, consent, contractual necessity, or other lawful bases available under applicable law.

19. Children

The Platform is not directed to children and is intended only for users who are at least 18 years old or the age of legal majority in their jurisdiction.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact support through the Help form and we will take appropriate action.

20. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, complete, update, delete, export, restrict, object to, or withdraw consent for certain processing of your personal information. You may also have rights to grievance redressal, nomination, complaint to a regulator, non-discrimination, or limits on certain sensitive-data uses.

To exercise rights, contact us through the Help contact form and include the relevant account email and request details. We may verify your identity before acting. Some rights are subject to exceptions, such as retention required by law, security needs, payment records, fraud prevention, or defense of legal claims.

If you are in India, DPDP rights may include access to information, correction, completion, updating, erasure, grievance redressal, and nomination. If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, GDPR-style rights may include information, access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and rights related to solely automated decisions. If you are in California, CCPA rights may include knowing, deleting, correcting, opting out of sale or sharing, limiting sensitive-information use, and non-discrimination where the law applies.

21. Marketing, Service Messages, and Public Content

We may send service messages, security alerts, billing notices, plan notices, support replies, legal update notices, and transactional notifications. You cannot opt out of messages necessary to operate or secure the Platform while you keep an active account.

Marketing messages, where used, are sent only where permitted by law and can be opted out of. Channel Posts are content you cause to be published from your account to Telegram or another destination; they are not marketing by tradign.com to your audience.

22. Changes and Contact

We may update this Privacy Policy when the product, processors, integrations, payment flows, legal requirements, or security practices change. Material changes may be shown in-app, sent by email, or both. Where applicable law requires consent, we will request it.

For privacy requests, billing privacy questions, support issues, legal questions, or account-data requests, use the Help contact form and include the relevant account email, route, screenshots if useful, and a clear description of the request.
